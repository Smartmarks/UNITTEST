Class XI – Physics

Unit Test: Motion in a Plane & Gravitation

Maximum Marks: 25 Time: 45 minutes

General Instructions:

  1. All questions are compulsory.
  2. Draw diagrams wherever necessary.
  3. Use symbols and units properly.
  4. Give calculations up to correct significant figures.

Section A

Very Short Answer Questions (1 × 5 = 5 Marks)

1.

Write the expression for the horizontal range of a projectile in terms of its velocity and angle of projection.

2.

What is the angle between the velocity and acceleration vectors in uniform circular motion?

3.

State Kepler’s third law of planetary motion.

4.

Define gravitational potential energy.

5.

Write the SI unit of the gravitational constant (G).

Section B

Short Answer Questions (2 × 3 = 6 Marks)

6.

A projectile is fired with a velocity of 10 m/s at an angle of 30° with the horizontal.

  1. Find the time of flight.
  2. Find the maximum height reached.
(Take g = 10 m/s2)

7.

Two particles of masses 10 kg and 20 kg are separated by a distance of 1 m. Find the gravitational force on each due to the other.

8.

A stone is tied to a string and whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 2 m at a speed of 4 m/s. Find

  1. the centripetal acceleration, and
  2. the tension in the string if the mass of the stone is 0.2 kg.

Section C

Short Answer Questions – II (3 × 3 = 9 Marks)

9.

Derive the expression for the gravitational potential energy of a system of two point masses separated by distance r.

10.

The acceleration due to gravity on the surface of the Moon is 1.6 m/s2 and its radius is 1.7 × 106 m. Find the mass of the Moon.
(Take G = 6.67 × 10-11 Nm2/kg2)

11.

Derive the formula for the maximum height and range of a projectile in terms of initial velocity v0, angle of projection θ0, and acceleration due to gravity g.

Section D

Long Answer Question (1 × 5 = 5 Marks)

12.
  1. State Newton’s law of gravitation and discuss how it differs from Coulomb’s law.
  2. Derive an expression for the acceleration due to gravity (g) at a height h above the Earth’s surface in terms of g, Re, and h.
  3. If g = 9.8 m/s2 on the surface and Re = 6.4 × 106 m, find g at a height of 640 km.
